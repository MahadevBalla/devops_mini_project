# SEBI Mutual Fund Categories (India)

## Equity Funds

- Large Cap: invest minimum 80% in top 100 companies by market cap. Lower volatility.
- Mid Cap: invest minimum 65% in 101st–250th companies. Higher return potential, higher risk.
- Small Cap: invest minimum 65% in 251st company onward. Highest risk/return.
- Flexi Cap: invest minimum 65% in equity, no restriction on market cap. Fund manager discretion.
- Multi Cap: minimum 25% each in large, mid, small cap. Mandated diversification.
- ELSS: Equity Linked Saving Scheme — 3-year lock-in, 80C eligible.

## Debt Funds

- Liquid Funds: up to 91-day maturity instruments. Ideal for emergency fund, parking surplus.
- Short Duration: 1–3 year Macaulay duration. Stable returns, low interest rate risk.
- Gilt Funds: invest in government securities. Safe on credit risk, high on interest rate risk.
- Credit Risk Funds: invest in lower-rated bonds for higher yield. Higher default risk.

## Hybrid Funds

- Aggressive Hybrid: 65–80% equity + 20–35% debt. Good for moderate risk investors.
- Balanced Advantage / Dynamic Asset Allocation: changes equity/debt ratio dynamically.
- Arbitrage Funds: exploit cash-futures price difference. Equity taxation, low risk.

## Index Funds & ETFs

- Nifty 50 Index Fund: tracks Nifty 50. Lowest expense ratio (~0.1–0.2%). Benchmark for large cap.
- Nifty Next 50: tracks 51st–100th companies. Mid-large blend.
- TER (Total Expense Ratio): annual cost charged by AMC. Direct plans have lower TER than regular.

## Key Ratios

- XIRR: annualised return for SIP investments with irregular cashflows. More accurate than CAGR for SIPs.
- Expense Ratio: annual % charged by fund. Directly reduces returns.
- Sharpe Ratio: return per unit of risk. Higher is better.
- Alpha: return above benchmark. Positive alpha = fund manager added value.
