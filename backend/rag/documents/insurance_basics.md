# Insurance Basics for Indians

## Term Life Insurance

- Pure protection — pays lump sum to nominees if policyholder dies during term.
- No maturity benefit (unlike endowment/ULIPs — avoid those for pure protection).
- Recommended cover: 10–15x annual income. Add outstanding loans to this.
- Premium: ₹5,000–₹15,000/year for ₹1 Cr cover for a 30-year-old non-smoker.
- Claim settlement ratio: check IRDAI annual report. Prefer insurers with >98%.
- Online term plans are significantly cheaper than offline/agent-sold plans.
- Riders worth considering: accidental death benefit, critical illness, waiver of premium.

## Health Insurance

- Mediclaim / Family Floater: covers hospitalisation expenses. Sum insured shared across family.
- Individual policy: each member has their own sum insured. Better for older members.
- Super top-up: kicks in after base cover exhausted. Cost-effective way to increase cover.
- Minimum recommended: ₹5 lakh individual, ₹10 lakh family floater in metro cities.
- Pre-existing disease waiting period: typically 2–4 years.
- 80D deduction: ₹25,000 premium for self/family (₹50,000 if senior citizen). Old regime only.

## What to Avoid

- Endowment plans: combine insurance + savings at poor returns (~4–5%). Better to buy term + invest separately.
- ULIPs: high charges in early years eat returns. Lock-in 5 years. Avoid unless specifically needed.
- Credit card / home loan insurance pushed by banks: usually overpriced. Buy standalone term instead.

## IRDAI Guidelines

- Free-look period: 15 days (30 days for distance marketing) to return a policy.
- Moratorium period: after 8 continuous years, insurer cannot reject claim on non-disclosure.
- Cashless claims: available at network hospitals. Reimbursement for others.
