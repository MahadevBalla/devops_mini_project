# Budget 2025 — Key Highlights for Individual Taxpayers

## New Tax Regime Changes (Effective FY 2025-26)

- Zero tax for income up to ₹12,00,000 under new regime (rebate u/s 87A, max ₹60,000).
- New standard deduction increased to ₹75,000 (was ₹50,000).
- New slabs: ₹0–4L nil, ₹4–8L 5%, ₹8–12L 10%, ₹12–16L 15%, ₹16–20L 20%, ₹20–24L 25%, above ₹24L 30%.
- New regime is now the default regime from FY 2023-24 onward.

## Capital Gains Changes

- LTCG exemption raised to ₹1,25,000 (Section 112A) for listed equity and equity mutual funds.
- LTCG rate on equity: 12.5% beyond exemption (holding period > 12 months).
- STCG rate on equity: 20% (holding period ≤ 12 months, Section 111A).
- Debt mutual funds: taxed at slab rate regardless of holding period (no LTCG benefit).

## NPS Enhancement

- Employer contribution to NPS deductible up to 14% of basic salary (was 10%) under new regime.
- NPS 80CCD(1B): additional ₹50,000 deduction available under old regime only.

## Other Changes

- TDS threshold on interest income raised.
- No changes to 80C limit (remains ₹1,50,000) — only available under old regime.
- Home loan interest Section 24(b): ₹2,00,000 deduction only under old regime.
