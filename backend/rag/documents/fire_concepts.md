# FIRE — Financial Independence, Retire Early

## Core Concept

FIRE = accumulate a corpus large enough that investment returns cover all expenses indefinitely.
The target corpus is annual expenses divided by Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR).

## Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR)

- 4% rule: from Trinity Study (US equities). Withdraw 4% of corpus annually, portfolio survives 30 years.
- India adjustment: use 3.5% SWR conservatively — higher inflation (~6% vs 3%), shorter equity market history.
- Formula: FIRE corpus = Annual expenses / SWR. Example: ₹60,000/month expenses → ₹72L/year → corpus = ₹72L / 0.035 = ₹2.06 Cr.

## Types of FIRE

- Lean FIRE: minimal lifestyle, lower corpus target. High frugality required.
- Fat FIRE: comfortable lifestyle, larger corpus target.
- Barista FIRE: partially retire, cover basic expenses via part-time work, let corpus grow.
- Coast FIRE: accumulate enough early that compounding alone reaches target by retirement age — stop contributing.

## SIP Step-Up Strategy

- Increase SIP amount by 10% every year (inline with income growth).
- Dramatically reduces required starting SIP vs flat SIP.
- Example: ₹10,000/month flat SIP at 12% for 25 years → ₹1.89 Cr. Same SIP with 10% annual step-up → ₹3.5 Cr.

## XIRR vs CAGR for SIPs

- CAGR measures lump-sum investment return.
- XIRR accounts for timing of each SIP installment — correct metric for SIP performance.
- Nifty 50 TRI 10-year SIP XIRR historically: ~9.5% (conservative) to ~13% (optimistic).

## Inflation in FIRE Planning

- Must inflation-adjust retirement expenses. ₹50,000/month today = ~₹1.6L/month in 30 years at 4% inflation.
- Use nominal returns (not inflation-adjusted) for corpus growth, inflation-adjusted expenses for target calculation.

## Annuity Ladder (post-retirement)

- Don't keep everything in equity post-retirement.
- Common allocation: 50% equity (growth), 30% debt (stability), 20% liquid (2 years expenses as buffer).
