# NPS and EPF Guide — India

## NPS (National Pension System)

- Regulated by PFRDA. Available to all Indian citizens aged 18–70.
- Two tiers: Tier 1 (primary, tax benefits, withdrawal restricted) and Tier 2 (voluntary, no tax benefit, free withdrawal).
- Asset classes: Equity (E), Corporate Bonds (C), Government Securities (G), Alternative (A).
- Auto choice (lifecycle fund) reduces equity allocation with age automatically.
- Tax benefits: 80CCD(1) up to 10% of salary within overall 80C limit. 80CCD(1B): extra ₹50,000 only under old regime. 80CCD(2): employer contribution up to 14% of basic under new regime.
- Withdrawal: 60% lump sum tax-free at age 60; 40% must be used to buy annuity (taxable as income).
- Partial withdrawal allowed after 3 years for specific purposes (education, marriage, medical).
- PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) is portable across employers.

## EPF (Employee Provident Fund)

- Mandatory for employees earning ≤ ₹15,000/month basic salary.
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic salary. Employer: 12% (8.33% to EPS pension scheme, 3.67% to EPF).
- Interest rate set annually by government. FY 2024-25: 8.25%.
- Tax treatment: contributions under 80C, interest tax-free if ≤ ₹2.5L/year (₹5L for no employer contribution).
- Withdrawal: fully tax-free after 5 continuous years of service.
- UAN (Universal Account Number) links EPF across employers.

## NPS vs EPF vs PPF

| Feature | NPS | EPF | PPF |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Returns | Market-linked (~9–12%) | Fixed (8.25%) | Fixed (7.1%) |
| Lock-in | Till 60 | Till retirement/5yr | 15 years |
| Liquidity | Low | Medium | Low |
| Tax on maturity | Partial (40% annuity) | Fully tax-free (5yr+) | Fully tax-free |
| Employer contribution | Yes (80CCD(2)) | Yes | No |
