# India Income Tax Guide FY 2025-26

## Choosing a Regime

- New regime: better if total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + home loan) are less than ~₹3.75L.
- Old regime: better for salaried with home loan, HRA, and maxed 80C + NPS.
- Breakeven: if deductions > ₹3,75,000, old regime often saves more tax.

## Old Regime Deductions

- Section 80C (₹1,50,000): ELSS, PPF, EPF, LIC, home loan principal, children's tuition.
- Section 80D: health insurance premium — ₹25,000 self/family (₹50,000 if senior citizen), ₹25,000 parents (₹50,000 if senior citizen parents).
- NPS 80CCD(1B): additional ₹50,000 over and above 80C.
- Home loan interest Section 24(b): ₹2,00,000 for self-occupied property.
- Standard deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried.

## New Regime Deductions

- Standard deduction: ₹75,000 for salaried.
- Employer NPS contribution: up to 14% of basic (Section 80CCD(2)).
- No 80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest deductions allowed.

## Surcharge

- Applies when total income exceeds ₹50 lakh.
- Old regime: 10% (>50L), 15% (>1Cr), 25% (>2Cr), 37% (>5Cr).
- New regime: capped at 25% maximum (no 37% band).
- Health & Education Cess: 4% on (tax + surcharge) — both regimes.

## ELSS vs PPF vs NPS

- ELSS: 3-year lock-in, equity returns (~12% historical), 80C eligible.
- PPF: 15-year lock-in, 7.1% (government-set), 80C eligible, tax-free maturity.
- NPS: till age 60, market-linked, 80C + 80CCD(1B) eligible, partial taxable at maturity.
